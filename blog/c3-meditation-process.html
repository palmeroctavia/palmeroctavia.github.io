What did you think about meditation before reading chapter 2?

I was already an occasional practitioner of meditation before reading this chapter but my focus had been guided meditations. I had never really considered doing completely silent and relatively unstructured meditation before reading this chapter.

What new things have you learnt about meditation?

I already knew it was hugely beneficial in many areas of life but I had never thought of it as a way to practice delaying your responses to stimuli. This is a really helpful insight for me as it gives me a new thing to focus on while I meditate.

Did any of the suggested meditation techniques stand out to you?

I found the unstructured "focus on your breath" type of meditation very different to what I usually do. I actually found it helpful to not be distracted by music or guiding words and just to listen to myself and my body.

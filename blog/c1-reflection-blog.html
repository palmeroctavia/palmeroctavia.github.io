Five days in

14 July 2017





What's your take on the DBC/EDA experience?

The EDA experience is similar to a few experiences I've had before. I don't know if it's luck or destiny. Inspiring educational experiences seem to always occur at cross roads in my life. For example, I had a design teacher in high school who taught in a similar way to EDA. We as a group of learners had responsibility over our learning journey and she guided us when we needed a nudge. EDA has a similar feel, the learning journey is mine individually and collectively. What I do doesn't just effect me but also my cohort. This attitude matches my worldview. We are all essentially alone in this life journey. Relationships and collective ties are what keep us tethered to purpose, meaning and strength.

What are your impressions?

My impressions are that this cohort are all here to pursue our passions. We also all seem to want to learn the tools to use to turn this passion into a living. I really liked how people in the orientation session on Monday shared that they love gaming and Marvel. Life should be fun and I feel like this course will be fun (not in an easy sense). Instead, fun in a way that carries us all to a place of fulfilment.

How do you see yourself engaging with this type of culture?

I see myself thriving in the collective spirit of EDA. I suit autonomy, I have always felt most content and purposeful when I am:

  • doing something challenging;
  • for the common good; and
  • something that allows me to figure out my own way.

Have your expectations of EDA changed? If so, how?

My expectations haven't really changed. I knew the culture suits how I like to work. I also knew that learning so much content in a short space of time would be a challenge. I think the thing that has intrigued me is how it feels to be in a cohort of mostly males. It is a different energy to what I am used to and so I think it will take some adjusting to.

Are you excited to participate in this kind of learning environment? Does it make you nervous?

I am super excited to participate in this learning environment. It makes me nervous in a 'butterflies in stomach' way rather than dread. I think this is a journey that I have much to learn from and to give back to as well.
